KISAPMATA @ 4pm

Dir. by Mike de Leon

Over the course of an unrelenting 100 minutes, director Mike de Leon and co. takes you into the claustrophobic space that is the Carandang home. Its partriach, a retired police officer, Sgt. Carandang grasps onto his home with tight grip. His daughter Mila intends to marry the father of her child, to which her father allows on the condition of an expensice dowry. Once they marry, the film depicts the cyclical horrors of its household as its patriarch treis to control everyone in his family, even as they try to escape.

De Leon and co. adapt a Nick Joaquin news report about a familicide to craft a tale that plays equal parts as a stirring, terrifying horror film, and an allegory for the horrors and oppressions of Philippine people under martial law. Kisapmata is masterfully crafted as the film ebbs to the pacings of hybrid lucid dreams and realities.

cw: abuse (physical, psychological, emotional, domestic), overt references to sexual abuse and incest, violence, familicide, police brutality, parental control, blood.

PAN DE SALAWAL @ 7pm

Dir. by Che Espiritu

BELIVE IN MIRACLES.

A special little homeless girl travels about the Philippines curing the afflicted with her mysterious healing powers, until one day she befriends a sick old man who for some perplexing reason she is unable to cure. The unlikely pair are drawn to each other and their lives are forever changed by the unique frienship and magical journey together.

cw: depression
